Idly Rice

About Idly Rice

Idly rice, also known as parboiled rice, is a variety specifically cultivated and processed for making idlis, a popular South Indian steamed cake. Here's a brief overview of idly rice:

Characteristics:

  • Processing: Idly rice undergoes parboiling, a process where the rice is partially boiled in the husk. This makes the rice firmer and less sticky compared to regular white rice.
  • Texture: It has a slightly harder texture that, when ground, produces a batter with the right consistency and stickiness needed for idlis.
  • Starch Content: High starch content aids in the fermentation process, which is crucial for making soft and fluffy idlis.

Usage:

  • Idli Preparation
  • Dosa and Other Dishes
